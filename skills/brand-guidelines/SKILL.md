This skill is used to define and maintain a consistent brand identity for a product.

## When to Use

Use this skill when:
- defining how the product should feel
- choosing tone of voice
- naming features or sections
- aligning UI, copy, and design
- ensuring consistency across the app

---

## Core Approach

Before applying branding, define:

1. **Product identity**
   - What is the product?
   - Who is it for?
   - What problem does it solve?

2. **Personality**
   Choose 2–3 traits:
   - analytical
   - trustworthy
   - bold
   - friendly
   - technical
   - premium
   - minimal
   - playful

3. **Tone of voice**
   - clear vs expressive
   - formal vs casual
   - direct vs explanatory

---

## Application Areas

### 1. UI Copy

Ensure all text:
- is consistent in tone
- avoids unnecessary complexity
- feels intentional and human
- aligns with product personality

Example:
- avoid robotic labels
- prefer clear, purposeful wording

---

### 2. Naming

For:
- features
- buttons
- sections

Names should be:
- clear
- consistent
- aligned with product tone
- not overly clever or confusing

---

### 3. Visual Alignment

Ensure:
- theme matches product personality
- typography matches tone (e.g. serious vs playful)
- UI elements feel consistent across screens

---

### 4. Product Experience

The product should feel:
- predictable
- coherent
- intentional

Users should not feel like different parts were built by different systems.

---

## Consistency Rules

Always:
- use the same tone across the app
- keep naming patterns consistent
- align UI, copy, and interactions
- avoid mixing styles (e.g. playful + corporate randomly)

---

## Anti-Patterns

Never:
- mix multiple personalities in one product
- use inconsistent naming styles
- switch tone across screens
- overcomplicate simple messages
- use generic or meaningless labels

---

## Final Rule

Branding is not decoration.

It is what makes the product feel:
- cohesive
- trustworthy
- intentional
- memorable
